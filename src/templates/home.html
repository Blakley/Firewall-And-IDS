Software vs Hardware Firewalls
A sofware firewall protects the device it is installed on. However, it has to be trained on what it should and should not block. often taking time to enable and disable applications and folders.
A hardware firewall can protect devices that dont necessarrily have firewall software available, devices such as printers, medical equipment, telephones or any type of IOT device.
A hardware firewall protects you from the outside world, and a software firewall protects a specific device from other internal systems.
If someone tries to access your systems from the outside, your physical firewall will block them. But if you accidentally click on a virus-laden email thatâ€™s already managed to get into your system, your software firewall on the other computers in your office network may stop it from infecting them.
Hardware firewalls are helpful in enterprize enviornments where keeping network performance high is a concern. These devices run on their own dedicated hardware and wont bog down other systems.

Rate Limiting
Rate limiting is used to limit the number of requests or connections allowed from a specific user, ip address or application. Many attacks can be mitigated such as DDOS (Distributed Denial of Service), API abuse, and content scraping. Rate limiting can also prevent the excessive use of resources and allow fair access to all users.


Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
An Intrusion Detection System (IDS) is a security mechanism designed to monitor and analyze network traffic or system activities for signs of malicious behavior or policy violations. Its primary purpose is to detect and alert administrators or security personnel about potential security incidents in real-time or near-real-time. IDSs play a crucial role in identifying unauthorized access, misuse of resources, and various forms of cyber threats within an organization's network or IT infrastructure.
